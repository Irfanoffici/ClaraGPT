Hypertension (high blood pressure): A condition where the force of blood against artery walls is too high. Normal BP is below 120/80 mmHg. Stage 1 hypertension is 130-139/80-89 mmHg. Treatment includes lifestyle changes and medications like ACE inhibitors.

Diabetes Type 2: A chronic condition that affects how your body processes blood sugar. Symptoms include increased thirst, frequent urination, and blurred vision. Management involves diet, exercise, and medications like metformin.

Influenza (Flu): A viral infection that attacks your respiratory system. Symptoms include fever, cough, sore throat, muscle aches, and fatigue. Prevention includes annual vaccination and good hygiene practices.

Asthma: A condition where your airways narrow and swell, producing extra mucus. This can make breathing difficult. Common triggers include allergens, exercise, and respiratory infections. Treatment involves inhalers and avoiding triggers.
